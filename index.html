Biography

Tupac Amaru Shakur was born in New York City in 1971 on June 16, Tupac grew up primarily in Harlem. In 1984, his family moved to Baltimore, Maryland where he became good friends with Jada Pinkett Smith. His family moved again in 1988 to Oakland, California. His first breakthrough in music came in 1991 as a member of the group Digital Underground. In 1996 Tupac Amaru Shakur Shakur was shot four times, including in the chest, and died from his injuries six days later. He was 25. The rapper, who chronicled his hardships and wove introspective lyrics around themes of poverty, police brutality and self-proclaimed "thug life," had endured previous violence.
